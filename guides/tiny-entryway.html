Small-space guide

A tiny entryway that works harder

A useful entryway is a landing zone, not a showroom. Give the things you drop every day an obvious place to go.

I may earn a commission from qualifying purchases through links in this guide.

Build your landing zone

  1. Pick one shallow tray for keys, earbuds, and the small items that normally scatter.
  2. Give frequently used bags one hook each. Keep the floor as clear as possible.
  3. Limit shoes at the door to the pairs in your current rotation.
  4. Add a narrow basket for incoming mail and empty it on a fixed day each week.
  5. Use vertical space for umbrellas, reusable shopping bags, or dog-walking supplies.
Optional tray to consider:

A small catch-all tray can create one visible home for keys, a wallet, and loose everyday items. Check the tray dimensions against your entryway surface.
