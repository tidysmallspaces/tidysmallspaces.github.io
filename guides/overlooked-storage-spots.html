Use what you have

Three overlooked storage spots to check first

Before buying another bin, look for unused areas that are already part of your home. The goal is to solve a storage problem without making the room feel fuller.

I may earn a commission from qualifying purchases through links in this guide.

Walk through these three zones

  1. Behind doors: useful for lightweight, frequently used items that otherwise land on the floor.
  2. Under furniture: helpful for low containers when the pull-out path stays clear.
  3. Above eye level: best for labeled categories that you reach for occasionally.
Optional organizer to consider:

A hanging over-door organizer can help use the first overlooked zone without drilling or occupying floor space. Confirm door clearance, dimensions, and intended load.

Do not fill every empty area

Some open space is valuable. Use hidden areas selectively and stop when the daily clutter problem is solved.
